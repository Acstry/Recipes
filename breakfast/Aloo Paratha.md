### Ingredients:
Dough
- 2 cups whole wheat flour
- 1/4 tsp salt
- 1 tbsp oil
- water, as needed
Filling
- 350g potatoes
- 1 green chili, chopped
- 3/4 tsp grated ginger
- 2 tbsp coriander leaves, chopped fine
- 1/2 tsp salt
- 1/2 tsp garam masala
- 1/2 tsp kashmiri red chili powder
- 1 tbsp lemon juice
- 1 tsp kasuri methi (dried fenugreek leaves)
- 4 tbsp ghee, to fry

### Recipe:
1. Boil potatoes until fork tender. 
2. While potatoes boil, make the dough. Add flour, oil, and salt to a bowl. Pour 1/2 cup water and begin to mix to form a dough. Pour more water as needed to make a soft, non-sticky dough. 
3. Knead well until soft and pliable. Cover and rest until filling is ready. 
4. Allow potatoes to cool slightly. Grate or mash well until no bits remain. Do not over-mash as potatoes will turn pasty and sticky. 
5. Add remaining seasonings and mix. Taste and adjust seasonings. 
6. Divide aloo stuffing and dough into eight parts. Keep dough covered.
7. Roll dough ball in palms until smooth. On a lightly floured surface, flatten ball and dip in some flour. 
8. Roll out a 5in roti and place stuffing in center. Hold edges and make small pleats, bringing together and joining until top is sealed. Remove excess dough from top.
9. Dip stuffed ball in flour and flatten gently to spread filling evenly. Dust off excess flour. 
10. On floured surface, place disc on rolling area, sealed side down. Begin to roll evenly without applying too much pressure. 
11. Rotate paratha continuously every 4-6 rolls to ensure it does not tear or stick. If you see stuffing come out, gently apply flour to seal. Roll as thin or thick as desire without tearing. 
12. Heat a pan or griddle. Add in paratha. Within 2-3 mins you will see bubbles. At this point, drizzle with ghee or oil and flip. 
13. Press down edges to cook thoroughly. Paratha is finished when it is golden brown. 
14. Top with butter and serve with pickle or yogurt.
